Principles

The token should be usable and have the intrinsic characteristics of a consumer token.

  1. Ensure the token’s intrinsic features do not include equity ownership, a predominant interest in a fund, passive-earned dividend rights, or other characteristics of a financial instrument.
  2. Ensure the token is consumptive in nature—i.e., the token represents or provides access to a good, service, and/or content.
  3. Ensure the token is usable as intended.

The project should have an appropriate and transparent governance structure.

  1. Define an appropriate organizational and governance structure for the project and review it periodically for improvement.
  2. Ensure key people involved in the project are qualified for their role.

Token distributions should be orderly, fair, and carried out in a transparent manner.

  1. Minimize or eliminate the following practices:
    • Token bonuses, discounts, and other preferential terms—especially based on the wealth or purchasing power of buyers.
    • Distribution of tokens to individuals who do not understand the token’s functionality or do not intend to use the token.
    • Selling tokens that exceed the cost and risk that a buyer can reasonably bear.
  2. Ensure the terms of all token distributions are transparent.

The purpose of the token distribution and expected plans for any proceeds should be transparent.

  1. Define the expected use of token proceeds.
  2. Ensure project resources are only used for project purposes and not for personal gain.
  3. Reevaluate and update plans based on project progress.

Projects should define token supply parameters and prudently manage retained inventory.

  1. Define the rules that will govern total token supply—i.e., how new tokens can be created or existing tokens can be destroyed.
  2. Define the factors that may impact the number of tokens in circulation—e.g., lockup periods and transfer restrictions.
  3. Define the governance for token inventory that is retained by the project or related entities.

The project should strive to create a level playing field in any secondary markets.

  1. The project should identify, manage, and mitigate conflicts of interest.
  2. Implement measures to prevent the trading of tokens based on material, non-public, and confidential information.
  3. Avoid practices that are designed to improperly or artificially manipulate the price of tokens.

Any tokens offered to consumers should be safe to use and own.

  1. Ensure the project has passed a sufficient third-party audit of all relevant smart contracts.
  2. Implement effective mechanisms for the safety and security of the token and project.
  3. Inform consumers promptly of any security risks or vulnerabilities in the technology.

Marketing should be fair and accurately reflect the project and product.

  1. Ensure all product advertising is fair and avoids misleading customers.
  2. Avoid marketing the token as a financial investment.
  3. Avoid marketing the ability of buyers to re-trade the token for passive financial profit on speculative secondary markets.

The project should protect and empower its customers.

  1. Implement effective mechanisms for redressing or refunding customers, in whole or part, as appropriate, including if the project is canceled or abandoned.
  2. Implement effective mechanisms for customers to cancel their purchase and get a refund, in whole or part, including as may be required by local law.
  3. Implement a privacy policy related to customer data.
  4. Implement effective mechanisms for customers to be recognized or receive value for their contributions to the project.
  5. Confirm responsibilities and expectations of purchasers in one or more legally binding documents.

The project and related organizations should engage in reasonable best efforts (in a complex and currently evolving regulatory environment) to comply with applicable laws, including securities, data privacy, anti-money laundering, and tax laws.

  1. Comply with securities regulations in applicable jurisdictions.
  2. Comply with applicable tax laws.
  3. Comply with applicable data protection and privacy laws (e.g., GDPR).
  4. Comply with applicable know-your-customer (KYC) and anti-money laundering laws.
